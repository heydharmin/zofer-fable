Why a council

One reviewer misses things. Ten just argue.

A single generalist has one set of blind spots. But the obvious fix — a room full of agents debating freely — collapses into mush or runs forever. The council keeps what works about a crit and cuts what doesn't.

✕ Naive version
10 agents debate endlessly · humans rate every raw idea · opinions with no evidence
✓ The council
Lenses generate in parallel · one critique round with a scoring judge · rate only the top 6 · every finding cites a real case study
The pipeline

Five phases, wide to narrow

Everything grounds in the real screen. The flow fans out to generate, then filters ruthlessly — the orange step is where many findings become one design.

01

Frame

Read the real component. Pin the goal, the user, the constraints, and exactly what ships today — no imagined UI.

02

Diverge

Four lenses run in parallel, each proposing from a viewpoint the others can't see.

03

Ground & critique

Each finding is attacked once: is it real? A case study must support or refute it — or say “none found.”

04

Score

Survivors ranked by Impact × Confidence × Ease. Top six shortlisted — deterministically, in code.

05

Synthesize

The winners become one coherent redesign, an ASCII wireframe comparison, and a prioritized backlog.

The lenses

Four voices that see different things

Each is a standalone agent with one job and one bias. Distinct on purpose — overlap would just be the same finding four times.

Lens 01

Conversion

Friction on the path to the goal — dead steps, weak CTAs, surprise costs, forms that make you retype.

Catches: the leak between intent and done
Lens 02

Usability

Nielsen's heuristics and the states everyone skips — empty, loading, error, double-submit.

Catches: what breaks on the unhappy path
Lens 03

Accessibility

WCAG 2.2 AA for real assistive tech — contrast, keyboard, focus, labels, target size, semantics.

Catches: who's locked out entirely
Lens 04

Trust & persuasion

Whether reassurance sits where the risk is felt — social proof, escrow signals, honest totals, no dark patterns.

Catches: the doubt at the moment of commit

Impact × Confidence × Ease

Three cheap-to-judge signals, multiplied into one comparable score from 1 to 1000. High impact but low confidence? It drops. Great idea that's a nightmare to build? It waits.

The math is deterministic and runs in code — the agents judge, the ranking doesn't get a vote. Then you rate only the top six, and the redesign can re-weight to your call.

ICE = impact × confidence × ease → rank → top 6
