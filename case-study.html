01 · The problem

A great catalog behind a weak front door

The app had genuinely differentiated services — but the Home screen hid almost all of them and gave a first-time user no reason to trust it with their own car.

🗂 Buried catalog

School runs, Airport, Pet-to-vet, Intercity, the subscription ladder — all one tab away. Home showed 2 unpriced cards + a thin 3-option sheet that duplicated the Services tab.

🙈 Hidden prices

Only 2 of ~15 services showed a fare anywhere. Users had to tap in blind — and surprise cost is the #1 abandonment driver (Baymard).

👤 Faceless drivers

Blank illustration faces, zero social proof — on a service where a stranger drives your own car, and sometimes your kids.

✂️ Broken polish

Headings clipped mid-word (“Chauffeu”, “Personai Chauffeur”); unlabeled icon controls — on a brand selling luxury.

03 · The insight

Turn the weakness into the moat

“The relationship isn’t browsed pre-ride — it’s earned post-ride.”

Nobody wants to pick between twelve strangers; everybody wants to keep the driver who just proved himself. So the faceless-driver weakness becomes the emotional core: a named, rated, rebookable chauffeur who becomes your regular. The funnel writes itself — one-off → favorite → regular → subscription — and the north-star metric, repeat-driver rebooking rate, doubles as the social proof on the card.

And the skeptic's point is respected: browsing drivers kills ETA, so urgency gates the choice — on-demand stays auto-match; scheduled & recurring unlock “Choose your chauffeur.” Even Uber only allows a Favorite Driver inside Reserve.

07 · Success metrics & experiment

How I’d prove it works

North starRepeat-driver rebooking rate

Share of bookings made with a previously-used (“my regular”) chauffeur — the leading indicator of trust & retention.

Guardrail 1Booking-flow completion

Must not regress vs the current flow — added choice can’t add drop-off.

Guardrail 2Median time-to-assignment

Auto-match ETA must hold — choice can’t starve supply liquidity.

Primary metric: 28-day repeat-driver rebooking rate · Guardrails: completion, ETA · Secondary: choice-upgrade attach, tip rate, D30 retention.
Instrument: fork_viewed · choice_selected · driver_chosen · booking_completed · rebook_from_profile · made_regular

Two supporting tests: price-on-every-tile → Home tap-through; trust line + real photos → first-booking conversion.

09 · Reflection

Risks, trade-offs & what I’d do next

  • Supply liquidity is the real risk. Choose-a-driver only works where lead time exists — that’s why it’s gated to scheduled/recurring, and why the +AED fee is a supply-shaping lever, waived for regulars.
  • Silent substitution is the churn event. Recurring plans must never swap your driver quietly — 12h notice, the backup’s full profile, accept/reschedule/credit. It’s Urban Company’s #1 complaint; we design it out.
  • Cold-start for new drivers. A choose-first model can starve new chauffeurs of trips — so first-timers auto-match (the relationship is earned post-ride), and new drivers surface via “fastest” and price.
  • What I’d validate next: the willingness-to-pay for choosing a driver, and whether streaks or membership drives more rebooking — both testable in the plan above.
10 · Method

How this was produced — the UX Council

Every decision survived an adversarial process, not a single opinion. Diverge wide, converge hard.

Diverge

Up to 10 specialist lenses critique each screen in parallel, blind to each other → 20–50 raw findings.

Critique & score

A skeptic attacks each finding, grounds it in a real case study, and scores it by ICE. Findings survive, narrow, or die.

Converge

Survivors synthesize into one redesign + wireframe + backlog. 4 sprints · 197 agents.
